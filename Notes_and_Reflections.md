#### 🔑 Key Concepts
- **Clustering:** An unsupervised machine learning technique used to group similar data points together without predefined labels.
- **K-Means:** A clustering algorithm that divides data into K groups by assigning points to the nearest cluster center and iteratively adjusting those centers.
- **Centroid:** The center point of a cluster, representing the average position of all data points within that group.
- **Scaling:** Ensures all features contribute equally and prevents large-value variables from dominating.

---

### 📊 Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering

#### 📊 Overview
This project applies K-Means clustering to segment customers based on behavioral and financial data. The goal is to uncover hidden patterns and provide actionable business insights.

---

#### 🧠 Problem
Businesses often have diverse customers but lack clear segmentation. Without grouping customers, it becomes difficult to target marketing strategies effectively.

---

#### 📁 Dataset
The dataset includes:
- Age
- Annual Income
- Spending Score
- Online Activity Score
- Visits per Month  

⚠️ Contains missing values → requires preprocessing

---

#### ⚙️ Process

1. **Data Cleaning**
   - Handled missing values using mean imputation

2. **Feature Selection**
   - Selected key behavioral and financial features

3. **Scaling**
   - Applied `StandardScaler`

4. **Clustering**
   - Applied K-Means (`K = 4`)

5. **Visualization**
   - Used scatter plots

---

#### 🔍 Results
The clustering revealed 4 distinct customer segments:

- **Cluster 0:** High income, high spending → Premium customers  
- **Cluster 1:** Mid income, high spending → Digital-heavy customers  
- **Cluster 2:** Low income, frequent visits → Conversion opportunity  
- **Cluster 3:** High income, low spending → High-potential segment  

---

#### 💡 Business Insights
- Retain premium customers through loyalty programs  
- Target digital users via online marketing  
- Convert frequent low-spenders with promotions  
- Upsell high-income low-spenders  

---

#### 🧠 Key Learnings
- Real-world data is messy → preprocessing is essential  
- Clustering reveals hidden patterns  
- Visualization alone is not enough—interpretation matters  
- Clusters may not be perfect, but they are still useful  
- Not all clusters are perfectly separated, but they are still useful
